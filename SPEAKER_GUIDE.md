# Speaker Guide

Congratulations on getting your Pop-up Meetup talk approved! Here's everything you need to know to get ready.

## What Happens After Approval

1. A RevolutionVA team member will comment on your proposal issue with next steps.
2. We'll work with you to match your talk to an available venue and agree on a date.
3. Once confirmed, we'll help you create the Meetup event on [757 Developers](https://www.meetup.com/757dev/) and list it on [757tech.org](https://757tech.org).
4. We'll share the Meetup link so you can promote it through your own channels.

## What RevolutionVA Provides

- Event listing on Meetup and 757tech.org
- Promotion through the 757 Tech Newsletter, Slack, and social media
- Coordination with the venue
- A point of contact if anything comes up before or during the event

## What You're Responsible For

- Showing up on time and prepared
- Ensuring that your venue is available on your preferred date and time
- Bringing your own laptop if required for your talk
- Notifying us as early as possible if you need to reschedule or cancel
- Following the [Code of Conduct](CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md)

## Day-of Tips

- Arrive at least 15 minutes early to test AV and get settled.
- If you're doing a live demo, have a backup plan (screenshots, recorded video) in case of technical issues.
- Invite people you know — Pop-up Meetups thrive on word-of-mouth.
- Engage the room: questions and discussion are encouraged.

## After the Event

We'd love to hear how it went. Feel free to:

- Share photos or a recap on LinkedIn, X/Twitter, or Bluesky and tag @revolutionconf
- Post in the 757 Tech Slack
- Leave a comment on your GitHub issue — we use feedback to improve the program

## Questions?

Open an issue or email us at team@revolutionva.org.
